## 🌐 What is a VPS and Why Use It?

If you’re new to VPS, here’s a quick explanation:

A VPS (Virtual Private Server) is like renting a small, private computer on the internet. You can run programs on it 24/7, which is useful for web servers, databases, or services like OpenClaw.

Using a VPS means:

- Your OpenClaw service runs independently from your personal computer.
- Your application stays online even if your computer is off.
- You can access OpenClaw remotely from anywhere.

clawcontrol simplifies how you connect and work with your VPS, so you don’t need to memorize technical commands.

## 🛠 Troubleshooting and Tips

If you run into issues, here are some common checks:

- Double-check your login details for your VPS.
- Verify your internet connection is stable.

- Restart clawcontrol and try connecting again.
